Penetration Testing - What is it?

Penetration Testing, or Pen Testing, is basically an authorized test of an organization's computer network. There are varying categories of pen tests, based on the amount of knowledge that the testers have regarding the network.

Three categories into which pen testing scenarios can be organized are black box, grey box, and white box. Black box tests occur when the testers have no internal knowledge of the network which they are testing. They have to start from scratch. White box tests occur when the testers have complete knowledge of the internal network. Grey box testing occurs somewhere in the middle.

Pen testing typically follows the following process:

  1. Planning and Preparation
  2. Information Gathering and Analysis
  3. Vulnerability Detection
  4. Penetration Attempt
  5. Analysis and Reporting
  6. Cleaning Up

When conducting a penetration test, researchers will often use a suite of tools and purpose-built Operating Systems. This portal assumes the use of Kali Linux. In the subtopics to the left, we will explore how to use the various tools that can be found within Kali.
