What is this?

Anyone who works in the tech world will know that the industry is constantly evolving. Combine this with the vast amount of knowledge that any professional, but particularly IT professionals must achieve, and you start to come up with a dizzying amount of information.

Many will either choose to just try to remember it all or rely on the people around them to remind themselves of anything they forget. If there's one thing that I've learned in the Army, it's the importance of writing down information, and redundancy. First of all, there's no way any normal person will be able to keep track of all of that information on their own, especially once things start to get stressful. And more importantly, if you do forget something or lose that notebook that you keep, you'd better have a backup or you're done with.

This is where my knowledge dump comes into play. This is my effort to organize various things that I have learned and will learn in a manner that is helpful to me and hopefully to anyone else who stubles across this site. As a web site, it will most likely always be available to me as an IT professional. And being hosted on Github, that guarantees that that there will be a copy of all of my knowledge online, as well as in my PC's respository pull. Redundancy!

Anyone who stumbles across this, I hope that you find it useful, and if you have any recommendations, please drop me a line either on Github or through the "About" section of this site. Thanks!
